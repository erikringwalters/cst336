Engineering

Users are able to see mechanical objects in front of them using augmented reality. This could help greatly with prototyping and installation.1 Rather than spending time and money on a physical component or machine, engineers are now able to create or modify their projects through an augmented reality device. In fact: the first official augmented reality program was created and used by Boeing in order to instruct their employees on how to install airplane parts.2
